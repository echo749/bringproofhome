the physical artifacts of human connection deserve to be kept.
artifacts from a life well-lived.

it starts the way most things do. with a problem we couldn't stop thinking about.

we go to shows. a lot of them. the kind of nights that leave a mark, where the crowd becomes something more than a collection of strangers, where the music does something to the space that's hard to put into words... a shared sense of presence. we keep going back because that feeling is rare, and we know it.

and we come home with artifacts: ticket stubs, wristbands, slaps, t-shirts we didn't need but couldn't leave behind, and posters printed for a single night, in a specific city, for a show that happened once and never will again. physical proof that it really happened; that it wasn't just a dream.

some posters get framed, some find a wall, but plenty get rolled back up and stored with the best of intentions for future display until the wall space runs out, or the poster doesn't quite fit vibe of the room, or framing costs are too high. and there they stay, corners quietly filling with years of memories that deserve to be relived.

countless conversations and social media posts told us we weren't the only ones with this dilemma.

we went looking for a solution, something that could actually do justice to what we're archiving. not just hold the posters, but protect them, display them, make them feel like the artifacts they are. something refined enough to leave on display, sturdy enough to last. we wanted it to look like it belonged, not like a workaround. we wanted something you could put out on the coffee table and leave there…a conversation piece.

we couldn't find it. so we built it.

the courier bag came from the same place: the lived experience of carrying a poster tube through a packed crowd all night. dancing, searching for somewhere to stash it so you could actually be present for the show. we've all done the shuffle. we wanted something designed for that specific moment, and every moment after it. and because the night rarely unfolds with only a poster tube in hand, we added webbing straps and d-rings for the jacket, the extra merch, whatever else you're carrying around and out of there.

this is what "proof." is, built by music people, for music people, for the nights that stay with you and the things you carry home from those nights: artifacts from the field.

returns & exchanges

standard returns

we accept returns within 30 days of delivery. items must be unused and in original packaging. buyer covers return shipping. once received and inspected, refunds are issued to the original payment method within 5–7 business days.

damaged or defective items

if your order arrives damaged or has a defect, we'll exchange it. no questions. send us a photo and your order details and we'll sort it out, including return shipping on our end. defective item claims must be made within 30 days of delivery.

custom cover orders

the chronicle. — customized is made to order. we send a digital mock-up for your approval before anything goes to production, so you know exactly what you're getting. because of this, custom orders are final sale. the only exception: if your order arrives damaged, we'll replace it.

how to start a return or exchange

use the contact form. include your order number and a description of the issue. for damaged items, attach a photo. we'll get back to you within two business days.
